Analising some songs


As diferenças entre os tipos de traduções podem ser vistas no filme da Disney “Encanto”, na versão em português do Brasil, na música “Não Falamos do Bruno” (do inglês: “We Don’t Talk About Bruno”). Podemos perceber numa certa parte da música a diferença de objetivos e preocupações de ambas as traduções. No áudio original, em inglês, o personagem Camilo canta “Isabela, your boyfriend's here”, que traduzido palavra por palavra fica “Isabela, seu namorado está aqui”. Na legendagem, ela foi colocada como “Isabela, seu namorado chegou”, que é bem próximo ao original. Já na dublagem para o português ela ficou como “Isabela, seu boy chegou”, onde “boy” é uma gíria utilizada para se referir ao namorado de alguém, ou seja, além de adaptar esse trecho para a cultura brasileira, os movimentos dos lábios do personagem se encaixam quase que perfeitamente, pois se utilizada a palavra “namorado” os movimentos seriam bastante diferentes do original “boyfriend”.


Analysis of "Saber quem sou"

Analyzing a little the Portuguese version of Brazil of the song "Saber quem sou" (from the original How Far I’ll Go) of the Disney children’s film "Moana" also finds some adaptations made for dubbing. In the original version we find the following excerpt "I’ve been Standing at the edge of the water; Long as I can Remember; Never really Knowing Why" which cannot be translated literally because the result would be a very long phrase for the melody and time of the song in Portuguese of Brazil, so the first sentence of the song that translated would be “Eu estive parada à beira da água” was “Aqui sempre, sempre à beira da água” where it was used a technique of repetition of the word "sempre" so that it could keep the rhythm and sung with the contraction "Sempra beira da água" not to leave the metric. In the second sentence the adaptation is smaller since it is caused by the withdrawal of a single word, "can". In Brazilian dubbing is “Desde quando eu me lembro” in a literal translation would be “Desde que eu consigo me lembrar”. In the chorus the adaptations are bigger, the first sentence "See the line Where the sky Meets the sea? It calls me" in dubbing is “O horizonte me pede para ir tão longe” makes sense this choice of words because the line where the sky meets the sea is the horizon, so the meaning of the phrase continues and the literal translation would not fit in the melody of the song.
The dubbed version brings more references to the context of the work that in a literal translation would not have, an example of this is in the excerpt from the chorus "If the Wind in my sail on the sea; Stays behind me" dubbed is “Se as ondas se abrirem pra mim de verdade” that references the scene from the beginning of the film where the sea opens to protagonist while she was still a child and gives him the heart of Tefiti. Also in "I Wish I could be the Perfect Daughter" is seen this influence, because in the voice acting is “Tento não causar nenhuma mágoa”, which refers to Moana’s concern not to disappoint her father and her people. This song was a great success and was translated into many languages. In the Spanish version, the excerpt in the original is "I Wish I could be the Perfect Daughter" is "Nunca la perfecta Niña he sido" and unlike the Brazilian version that shows the protagonist worried about hurting no one, this dubbing refers to the wishes and attempts of Moana since very young trying to go to sea and going against the wishes of the father.



Analysis of "Into the Unknown"

In the film Frozen II, in the song "Minha Intuição" ("Into the Unknown"), the translations performed, when compared side by side, are quite different, especially the dubbing, but it can be justified when we look at the context. Translating literally, the chorus "into the Unknown" would be "para o desconhecido", in the translation for the subtitle we have "rumo ao desconhecido", which did not change much of the original, only used a synonym for "para". In the case of dubbing we see a different and more complex situation, because the chorus was translated into "minha intuição". When seen like this, it is very distant from the original and even loses its meaning, but when we see the previous parts we can see that even if the translation is different, the sense/ purpose remains Analyzing the line “Everyone I've ever loved is here within these walls; I'm sorry, secret siren, but I'm blocking out your calls; I've had my adventure, I don't need something new; I'm afraid of what I'm risking if I follow you; Into the unknown”, you can see that his meaning would be that the character, even if she is being called by something to an unknown place, she wants to remain in the place she is with the people she loves, not wanting more changes and adventures in her life The dubbing follows this same sense by saying: “Todo mundo que eu amo está neste lugar; Lamento, voz secreta, mas eu vou te ignorar; Já fui muito longe, eu já me decidi; Só existe um caminho, e eu vou seguir; Minha intuição”. Here we see the meaning of the translation for "minha intuição", because intuition, that is, her desire at that moment is to stay with those she loves, without going out on new adventures. That said, in addition to maintaining the original purpose, this change made the translation fit the movements of the characters lips.
